SATISFACTION

COMMITMENT

HIGHEST
STANDARDS

HCCG recruits Harvard’s most capable and dedicated students and selects fewer than 10% of applicants. This talent, combined with training from industry professionals, underpins the impeccable standards of our work. Our members build on their skills and experience to join top employers.

UNPARALLELED
VALUE

Our group provides the best talent at competitive fees. Each team commits over 400 hours to a case over the course of 10 to 12 weeks to complete significant projects and produce impactful findings. Clients are consistently impressed with our work and report tangible results, with many returning for future engagements.

CUSTOMIZED
SOLUTIONS

HCCG tailors each case to best match clients’ goals and continually adapts as ideas develop and opportunities change. By working flexibly from the ground up instead of relying on generic frameworks, we foster innovative thinking and deliver actionable solutions that fit our clients' business plan.

UNIQUE
RESOURCES

Our group offers unmatched access to Harvard’s students, alumni, faculty, and research. Our position at the center of these people and resources produces unparalleled insights. Past cases have leveraged focus groups, alumni interviews, research databases, and faculty at HBS to create unique value.

TEAMWORK

The Harvard College Consulting Group uses an adaptable and customizable team structure and a mix of backgrounds and skills to deliver results. By combining several students with different skills, experiences, and academic backgrounds, HCCG case teams tackle each question from many perspectives. Each team is led by a Case Team Leader, who oversees the research and analysis of two Associates and three Analysts. Our technical and quantitative strength allows these teams of six undergraduates to support and test conclusions rigorously. Teams also receive specialized training once staffed to further refine the skills needed for the particular case. Our case teams typically deliver slide decks but have also produced written reports, quantitative models, and white papers in the past.
